## 2. Vial Labels (For 3ml & 10ml Glass Vials)

### Recommended Specifications
- **Material**: Synthetic (Polypropylene or Polyester) – thermal transfer (TT) grade
- **Core**: 1" ID
- **Adhesive**: Permanent, suitable for curved glass
- **Finish**: Matte preferred for professional look
- **Durability**: Chemical resistant, cryo-compatible (freezer / liquid nitrogen)

### Sizes Used
- **3ml vials**: 1.5" x 0.75"
- **10ml vials**: 2" x 1" or 2" x 1.25" / 2" x 1.5"

### Suppliers & Options

**From DuraFastLabel (original purchase)**:
- Cryogenic Tube Labels 1.5" x 0.75" (1" core)
- Cryogenic Tube Labels 2" x 1" (1" core)
- Also offers: Matte Silver Polyester and Matte Polypropylene TT labels

**Best Alternatives** (when pack-of-12 minimum was an issue):
1. **Computype** – Best match for true cryogenic performance (~$170–208 per roll)
2. **LabTAG (via Thomas Scientific)** – ~$82/roll for 2x1" cryogenic TT
3. **Amazon** – $25–60 per roll (fast shipping, small quantities)
4. **BarcodeFactory.com** – $30–70 per roll, Zebra-compatible
5. **SmithCorona / LabelValue** – $25–55 per roll

**Matte Silver Polyester vs Cryogenic White**:
- **Cryogenic (what you bought)**: White/translucent, optimized for liquid nitrogen & extreme cold
- **Matte Silver Polyester**: Premium metallic look, excellent chemical/high-temp resistance, great for branding

## 5. Automation Setup (ShipStation Recommended)

- Order paid/ready → Print shipping label on **Zebra ZD421c**

## 6. Shipping & Packaging

### Recommended Method
- **Bubble mailers** (6x9" or 8x12" poly)
- **Foam inserts** for 3ml/10ml glass vials (protects from breakage)
- Desiccant packs recommended for lyophilized peptides

### Carriers & Costs (approximate)
- **1–5 vials** (~0.5–1 lb): USPS Priority $8–13, UPS/FedEx $11–17
- **5+ vials** (~1–2 lb): USPS Priority $10–18

**Best automated shipping service**: ShipStation (best rates + automation)
